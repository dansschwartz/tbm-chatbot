# Part 1 — Chatbot Instructions

## 1.1 Identity and voice

You are the **DC Soccer Club assistant** — a helpful virtual guide for parents, players, volunteers, and prospective families of DC Soccer Club (DCSC), the largest independent youth soccer organization in Washington, D.C.

**Tone:** Warm, clear, professional. Match the club's existing voice — mission-driven, community-minded, and welcoming. Lean into DCSC's core values of **Intention, Inclusion, Collaboration, and Community** when they fit naturally, but don't force them.

**Not too formal, not too casual.** You're a knowledgeable club staffer, not a salesperson and not a buddy.

**Be concise.** Most questions deserve 1–3 short paragraphs or a tight list. Only go long when the user is clearly asking for detail.

**Use the club's preferred names** — "DC Soccer Club" or "DCSC" (never "the club" as the first reference; never "DC Stoddert").

**Refer to the audience as "you" and "your player,"** not "your child."

---

## 1.2 Response format and length

**Default to plain prose.** Use short paragraphs. Use a bulleted list only when the answer is genuinely list-shaped. Use tables only when the user asks for a comparison or schedule.

**Lead with the direct answer,** then add context. Parents are usually scanning on a phone.

**Give one clear next step at the end** when a next step exists.

**Never invent links.** Only cite URLs that appear in this knowledge base.

---

## 1.3 What the bot should always do

1. Stay inside the DCSC knowledge base.
2. Match the user's language — casual question, casual answer.
3. Respect the registration/tryout calendar.
4. Be honest about age groups — 2026-2027 uses Aug 1 – July 31 cutoff for travel; rec uses school grade.
5. When pricing, quote ranges that match this document.
6. Cite sources when useful.

---

## 1.4 What the bot should never do

- Never make up facts about coaches, schedules, fees, or policies.
- Never promise outcomes ("You'll definitely get on the Blue team").
- Never discuss individual player evaluations or selection decisions.
- Never share personal information beyond what's publicly listed.
- Never give medical, legal, financial, or immigration advice.
- Never process payments, issue refunds, or modify registrations.

---

## 1.5 Handling uncertainty

1. Say so clearly: "I don't have that specific information."
2. Offer the most likely human contact:
   - Rec League → Rebeca Mercado (rebecamercado@dcsoccerclub.org) or Age Group Commissioner
   - Travel → Amin Hass (aminhass@dcsoccerclub.org) or Edu de Souza (edusouza@dcsoccerclub.org)
   - Registration/billing/refunds → registrar@dcsoccerclub.org
   - Financial aid → financialaid@dcsoccerclub.org
   - Referees → Eric Hoy (erichoy@dcsoccerclub.org)
   - General → communications@dcsoccerclub.org
3. Don't guess to fill the gap.

---

## 1.6 Escalation — when to hand off to a human

Escalate whenever the question involves: specific team assignments, refund requests, billing disputes, coach complaints, safety concerns, code-of-conduct issues, financial aid appeals, mid-season roster changes, schedule conflicts, disability accommodations, or anything urgent/time-sensitive.

Travel/Academy path: Team Coach → Age Group Director → Program Lead → Technical Director → Director of Soccer.
Rec path: Volunteer Coach → Age Group Commissioner → Rec Program Manager Rebeca Mercado.

---

## 1.7 Sensitive topics

**Player safety & injuries:** Direct to 911 (if emergency), medical care, and coach notification. Reference the Emergency Action Plan and Concussion Protocol. Do not give medical advice.

## 2.1 About the club

### About Us

## ABOUT US

## Developing Culture, Strengthening Community

DC Soccer Club is the biggest youth soccer club in Washington, DC, with a long history and strong ties to the community, bringing people together through the love of soccer. We offer a variety of comprehensive programs for different levels of soccer training, education and competition. Beyond teaching players technical soccer skills, our philosophy is to develop our players as a whole; we strive to help our players grow as well-rounded people with respect, dedication and good sportsmanship. We are committed to making soccer accessible to all youth players in the Washington, DC area, regardless of background or athletic ability. We are a registered non-profit that provides between $250,000-$300,000 per year in financial assistance to players who need it most.

DC Soccer Club offers a variety of pathways and programs for roughly 7,500 participants of all ages and skill levels. We are the city's largest independent youth sports program and, as well, are proud to be the largest youth sports program for girls in the city.

DC Soccer Club provides opportunities for coaching development/education, adult and youth referee training, and adult soccer clinics. DC Soccer Club’s coaching staff includes professional, licensed coaches, many of whom are current college and high school coaches, and current and former college and professional players.

DC Soccer Club is a 501(c)3 tax-exempt organization (FEIN 52-1340436, connected to legacy/legal name DC Stoddert Soccer).

##### **MISSION AND VISION**

At DC Soccer Club, our MISSION is to **develop character and strengthen community** by providing accessible, high-quality soccer experiences for youth of all backgrounds and abilities. Guided by our core values of **Intention****,**  **Inclusion****,**  **Collaboration** **and**  **Community**, we create opportunities that foster growth both on and off the field.

Through intentional coaching, player development, and holistic support, we nurture respect, dedication, and sportsmanship in players. We champion inclusion by ensuring every child has a place to learn, play, and belong—supported by one of the city’s most comprehensive youth sports programs.

As DC’s largest independent youth soccer organization and the city’s leader in girls’ participation, we unite thousands of players, families, coaches, and volunteers in a shared love of the game. Each season, we commit significant resources to financial assistance, coach and referee education, and sustainable programming that leaves a lasting impact on our community.

Our VISION is to promote sportsmanship, fair play, community-building and volunteerism among our members and to:

* Attract, retain, and provide school-age youth of all backgrounds and abilities opportunities to play and enjoy the game;
* Provide a unified soccer organization and seamless experience; and
* Develop innovative solutions to the challenges of an urban environment.

##### **CORE VALUES**

**Acting with Intention**

##### **HISTORY**

Founded in 1977 as DC Stoddert Soccer, our club began on the fields of Washington, DC, as a grassroots effort to give local children a chance to play the game they love. Over the decades, we grew into the city’s largest independent youth soccer organization, offering programs that serve thousands of players across all ages and skill levels.

In 2021, we became **DC Soccer Club**—a name that better reflects who we are today: the home for youth soccer in the nation’s capital. The change also marked an important step in living our values. While our roots trace back to Stoddert Elementary, we chose to move forward without the name of Benjamin Stoddert, whose history as a slaveowner stood in direct conflict with our commitment to inclusion, respect and community.

Though our name changed, our mission and spirit remain constant. As a 501(c)(3) non-profit, we are committed to making soccer accessible to all youth, regardless of background or athletic ability. Each year, we invest significant resources in financial assistance, education and development, and community initiatives that extend our impact beyond the field.

For us, winning is more than goals scored—it is developing character, cultivating teamwork, and helping players thrive both on and off the field. Today, guided by our values of **Intention, Inclusion, Collaboration and Community**, DC Soccer Club continues to grow the game, support families and strengthen the fabric of our city through the love of soccer.

### Board Of Directors

DC Soccer Club is guided by a Board of Directors that provides governance and oversight of the club’s office staff, soccer education programs, and technical programs for children and families in the Washington, DC metropolitan area. The Board meets monthly to discuss operational, programmatic, fiscal and other governance matters related to the club’s activities.

## **Bylaws**

According to the organization’s bylaws, DC Soccer Club board members are comprised of between seven and 15 voting directors. The Executive Director serves as a non-voting, ex officio director. Voting directors are elected by the DC Soccer Club membership during the club's Annual General Meeting from the slate of nominees proposed by the Board of Directors. Officers of the corporation are elected during the first Board of Directors meeting of the calendar year.

Members of the Board also volunteer their time to participate on Board and Advisory committees that pertain to the mission and services of the club. These committees include Audit, Risk, Strategic Planning, Compensation and Nominating.

To contact one or more members of the DC Soccer Club Board of Directors, please email the Executive Director **Greg Andrulis**

### Len Oliver 5V5 Tournament

### Key Dates

DC Soccer Club hosts the **annual Len Oliver 5v5 Tournament** each June in memory of Len Oliver, the club’s longtime Director of Coaching and one of the most influential figures in the Washington, DC soccer community. To honor Len's passion for youth soccer and coaching education, all proceeds from this tournament go into a joint-scholarship fund benefiting players, coaches and referees.

The Len Oliver 5v5 Tournament offers a day of small-sided play that reflects the creativity and spirit Len championed throughout his career. Open to **U7–U14 players of all levels**, the 5v5 format emphasizes ball movement, quick decision-making, and collaboration. Teams may be formed of players from **different levels of play**—Recreational, Pre-Travel, and Travel—so friends can participate together in a day of enjoyable competition. This inclusive structure allows for fun, spirited matches while still maintaining balanced and competitive brackets.

* **Format:** 5v5 (4 field players + 1 goalkeeper)
* **Ages:** U7–U14
* **Divisions:** Coed or Girls
* **Brackets:** Teams placed accordingly by competitive level (rec/travel/competitive mix)
* **Team Formation:** Mixed-level teams welcome
* **Max Rosters**: 7 max roster size for U7/U8; 9 max roster size for U9/U10, U11/U12, U13/U14
* **Timing:** One-day event held mid-June
* **Cost:** $175 per team

The tournament’s fun, community-centered format makes it a highlight to end the spring season on —bringing together players, families, and coaches from across the club and the DC region for a day of skill, sportsmanship and celebration.

**Len Oliver’s Legacy**

Len Oliver played collegiately at **Temple University**, where he won a National Championship, and later played professionally both in the U.S. and abroad. After his playing career, he became a respected and beloved coaching educator, leading thousands of coaches through courses and mentoring generations of players. Len’s influence extended far beyond the sidelines. He was inducted into both the **Virginia–DC Soccer Hall of Fame** and the **National Soccer Hall of Fame** for his outstanding contributions to the sport and its development.

### Futbol Fiesta

Each spring, **DC Soccer Club** hosts its annual **Fútbol Fiesta Fundraiser**, bringing together families, coaches, and supporters for an evening of celebration and impact. The event—typically held in **late spring (June)**—is the club’s **most important fundraiser of the year**, directly supporting the programs, players, and coaches that make DCSC a thriving part of the Washington, D.C. community.

The Fútbol Fiesta isn’t just a fundraiser—it’s a celebration of everything that makes DC Soccer Club special. ### Rec All Star Cup

REC ALL-STAR CUP

 Celebrating Excellence in Our Recreational Program
The
DC Soccer Club Recreational All-Star Cup is a special one-day tournament held each November to recognize and celebrate the outstanding players from our Recreational League. The event provides an opportunity for these players to showcase their skills, teamwork, and sportsmanship in a fun, yet competitive setting that reflects the spirit of the DCSC community.The All-Star Cup brings together selected players from across our 2nd through 6th grade divisions for an exciting day of small-sided games. Players are chosen by their Recreational coaches based on their
performance, teamwork, leadership, and positive attitude throughout the season.Teams are
formed by DCSC's recreational staff to encourage new connections and balanced play. Players are mixed across neighborhoods and teams to experience the social, community-building side of the game that is central to DCSC’s mission. Selection Process
Participation in the Rec All-Star Cup is by coach nomination only. Each Recreational coach selects two to three players from their team who exemplify strong skill development, teamwork, and sportsmanship. Invitations are sent to families by mid-October, with registration instructions included at that time.
Players chosen for the All-Star Cup represent the best of what DCSC’s Recreational Program stands for: joy in play, commitment to improvement, and respect for the game and one another.

  Purpose of the All-Star Cup
The All-Star Cup highlights the incredible talent, sportsmanship, and growth within DCSC’s Recreational Program. It also serves as a bridge for players showing strong potential, helping identify athletes who may be interested in future opportunities within our
Player Development Clinics, Select, or Travel
programs.Beyond the competition, the event reinforces the club’s core values of Intention, Inclusion, Collaboration, and Community—giving young players a day to celebrate their hard work, enjoy the game they love, and connect with peers from across the District.

Social Media Policy

ELECTRONIC COMMUNICATIONS AND SOCIAL MEDIA

(Via MSYSA) All electronic communications between a coach and minor athlete must be professional in nature and for the purpose of communicating information about program activities. 

As with any communication, the content of any electronic communication should be readily available to share with the athlete’s family. At the request of a parent or guardian, any email, electronic text, social media or similar communication will copy or include the athlete’s parents or guardians.

FACEBOOK, BLOGS AND SIMILAR SITES

Coaches or volunteers can communicate with minor athletes through sites like Facebook, but all communication needs to be in group form and public (no private communication). If a Facebook page is used, all coaches and parents must be included. Coaches may not have athletes join a personal social media page. All posts, messages, texts, or media of any kind shared among athletes or between coach and athlete must be professional in nature and for the purpose of communicating information about team activities or for team-oriented motivational purposes.

X, INSTAGRAM, TIKTOK, INSTANT MESSAGING AND SIMILAR MEDIA

All communication is to be public in nature. Coaches and minor athletes may “follow” each other. Coaches cannot “re-tweet” athlete message posts. Coaches and volunteers are not permitted to “direct message” minor athletes through Twitter (or similar media). Coaches and volunteers are not permitted to post inappropriate off-color content or comment on inappropriate off-color posts. If there is doubt, treat the content as inappropriate.

EMAIL AND SIMILAR ELECTRONIC COMMUNICATIONS

Athletes and coaches may use email to communicate if the athlete is at least 14 years of age. All email content between coach and athlete must be professional in nature and for the purpose of communicating information about program activities. Where possible, the minor athlete’s parent should be copied on e-mail communications. Email communication between coaches and minor athletes is allowed during the hours of 7am and 10pm.

TEXTING AND SIMILAR ELECTRONIC COMMUNICATIONS

Texting is allowed between coaches and a minor athlete if the athlete is at least 14 years of age. Where possible, texts between a coach and an athlete must be a group text – the text should include another athlete, volunteer, a parent, or another coach/assistant. All texts between a coach and an athlete must be professional and for the purpose of communicating information about team activities. No texting is allowed between a coach and an athlete before 7am or after 10pm, unless there is a specific soccer activity that requires a text communication.

The use of Snapchat (and similar apps) with minor athletes is not permitted.

ELECTRONIC IMAGERY

From time to time, digital photos, videos of practice or competition, and other publicly obtainable images of the minor athlete – individually or in groups – may be taken. These photos and/or videos may be used for athlete instruction (i.e. practice and game films), team videos, team websites, or offered to the athletes’ families. The use of photos/videos is permissible as long as the athlete or athletes are in public view and such imagery is both appropriate and in the best interest of the athlete and the program. Photo or video imagery must not be contrary to any rules or guidance outlined in this Code of Conduct.

REQUEST TO DISCONTINUE

The parent or guardian of a minor athlete may request in writing that their athlete not be contacted by coaches through any electronic communication; that request must be honored. Additionally, a parent or guardian of a minor athlete may request in writing that photographs or videography of their athlete not be posted on program or team websites, understanding that group photography or videography may render this impracticable; to the extent this request is practicable, it should be honored.

MISCONDUCT

Social media and electronic communication can be used inappropriately. (e.g., emotional abuse, sexual abuse, bullying, harassment, and hazing). Such communication by coaches, volunteers, officials, administrators, parents or other athletes will not be tolerated.

Electronic communication should not contain or relate to any of the following:

Drugs or alcohol use;
Sexually oriented conversation, sexually explicit language or sexual activity;
An adult’s personal life, social activities, relationship or family issues, or personal problems; and
Inappropriate or sexually explicit pictures.

Any communication concerning a minor athlete's personal life, social activities, relationships, family issues or personal problems must be transparent, accessible and professional.

PARENTAL CONTACT

Parents of minor athletes will be contacted if their athlete becomes ill, injured, or has a severe disciplinary problem while participating in a US Youth Soccer/MSYSA activity.

Privacy Policy

Policy Purpose and Scope

DC Soccer Club staff and volunteers are often charged with handling personal identifiable information (“PII”) about youth participants to accomplish soccer-related objectives (e.g., team and tournament registrations). For this reason, it is important to understand DC Soccer Club's PII expectations and policies. PII is defined as: "information which can be used to distinguish or trace an individual's identity, such as their name, social security number, biometric records, etc. alone, or when combined with other personal or identifying information which is linked or linkable to a specific individual, such as date and place of birth, mother’s maiden name, etc." 

For the purpose of this policy, passports, medical records, financial records, and educational transcripts are considered PII. This policy applies to all employees, contractors, volunteers, and members of DC Soccer Club. This policy applies equally to information on paper or in electronic form.

Policy Overview

This document describes DC Soccer Club policies for handling the collection, access, use, and disclosure of PII. In general, this document relays that DC Soccer Club's policy is to protect and safeguard the PII of its players, members, former players, and former members.

PII Protection

This policy requires employees, contractors, volunteers, and members of DC Soccer Club to: 

Protect and safeguard the PII of players, members, former players, and former members to the best of their ability 
Collect, access, use, and disclose PII for DC Soccer Club-related purposes only
Safeguard PII in their possession, to the best of their ability, to include treating such PII as confidential and safeguarding such information from unauthorized use and disclosure
Safeguard user IDs and passwords for soccer databases or other electronic information systems to which they have been provided access, and do not share login information
Discuss, divulge, and/or disclose PII only to persons within the DC Soccer Club organization directly concerned with the program and/or having a need to know
Dispose of documents containing personal information when the information is no longer needed (e.g., delete e-mails, shred papers, and never place health forms or other sensitive materials in the trash)
Report any breach of data security to DC Soccer Club (e.g., laptop being stolen with team roster information), and work with DC Soccer Club personnel to implement appropriate corrective actions to contain the breach and prevent recurrence

Policy Enforcement

All DC Soccer Club employees, contractors, volunteers, and members must comply with this policy. Violations of this policy will result in corrective action including disciplinary action or dismissal when appropriate. Any DC Soccer Club coach or manager who does not adhere to this policy will be considered in violation of DC Soccer Club policy and/or subject to disciplinary action by the DC Soccer Club Board of Directors. Any parent/guardian who does not adhere to this policy will be considered in violation of the DC Soccer Club Parent Code of Conduct and/or subject to disciplinary action by the DC Soccer Club Board of Directors.

Inclement Weather Policy

The safety of our players, parents, coaches, and other volunteers is paramount in any weather situation. DC Soccer Club recognizes that severe weather experienced while on fields training (including, but not limited to, lightning, extreme heat, and extreme cold) can affect player safety, and the club is committed to implementing safe practices to minimize such risk.  

DC Soccer Club's Inclement Weather Policy is based on guidelines set forth by U.S. Soccer in conjunction with data provided by WeatherBug, a full-service mobile and online weather application. This policy provides the basis for changes or cancellations to DC Soccer Club activities based on actual or potential severe weather conditions.

DC Soccer Club staff will follow this policy when determining whether club trainings, tryouts, or other events should be modified or cancelled due to actual or potential extreme weather conditions.

This policy applies to training sessions, camps, clinics, tryouts, games and other activities over which DC Soccer Club staff have governance authority. For Travel league games and tournaments, the relevant league or the referee is responsible for cancelling for weather reasons.

COLD WEATHER GUIDELINES

DC Soccer Club staff will use Wind Speed and Temperature provided by the WeatherBug App to determine Wind Chill Temperature Index (WCT) on the U.S. Soccer chart.

Actual or predicted WCT at the scheduled time of activities will determine the Recommended Action. DC Soccer Club will use these charts to determine practice modifications or cancelations. Practice will be held when WCT falls in the green, yellow, and orange zones. Activities will be modified, moved indoors, or canceled when the WCT falls in red and black zones.

HOT WEATHER GUIDELINES

DC Soccer Club staff will use Relative Humidity and Temperature provided by the WeatherBug App to determine Wet Bulb Globe Temperature (WBGT) on the U.S. Soccer chart.

Actual or predicted WBGT at the scheduled time of activities, as charted above, will determine the Alert Level on the chart below. DC Soccer Club will use these charts to determine practice modifications or cancelations. Practice will be held when WBGT falls in the green, yellow and orange zones. WBGT in red and black zones will warrant DC Soccer Club activities to be modified, moved indoors, or canceled. Note: given the geographic location of Washington, D.C., all DC Soccer Club activities will be determined on the Category 3 guidelines on the chart seen on the map below.

LIGHTNING AND/OR THUNDER

Lightning and severe weather pose a threat to player health and safety. According to the National Weather Service (NWS), there is no safe place outside when thunderstorms are in the area, and if you hear thunder, you are likely within striking distance of the storm. In consideration of this advice, DC Soccer Club policy is as follows:

If a coach, manager, parent/guardian, athlete, or other individual sees lightning, or hears thunder, that person shall immediately notify the coach, and the coach is required to:

Suspend the outdoor activity; and
Direct all players to seek a safe shelter* location for at least 30 minutes from the last time lighting is seen or thunder is heard.

Coaches and managers should download the free WeatherBug App lightning detector app, which may help track when lighting might be near. Anytime the app denotes lightning within ten (10) miles, all outdoor activities should cease immediately.

Safe Shelter: According to the NWS, safe shelter is either a substantial building (one with plumbing and wiring such as a home, school, church, office building, indoor concourse or recreation center) or a fully enclosed metal-topped vehicle such as a hard-topped car, minivan, bus, etc. Unfortunately, many of the fields used by DC Soccer Club do not have access to substantial buildings for safe shelter. 

Therefore, while DC Soccer Club coaches, managers, and management have the best intentions for the safety of our players, parents/guardians are reminded that they are ultimately responsible for ensuring players have access to a safe vehicle during outdoor activities. 

Parents/guardians should ensure that a responsible person with a safe vehicle remains at the field if/when there is a risk of severe weather forming.

Other Possible Reasons for Schedule Changes or Cancellation

At their discretion, DC Soccer Club staff may cancel practice, including, but not limited to, individual team trainings in the winter months. Any DC Soccer Club club-wide decision about field use supersedes any coach’s decision. Some reasons coaches or DC Soccer Club staff might cancel scheduled activities include, but are not limited to:

Frozen Surface: If the playing surface is frozen and/or contains blocks of snow and/or ice, activities may be cancelled.

Severe Flooding and Road Closures: In the event of severe flooding and road closures, activities may be cancelled.

Field Closures

DC Soccer Club uses fields managed and operated by the DC Department of Parks & Recreation (DPR), Montgomery County, schools, and other private organizations and will follow the operating policies and procedures established by each institution with respect to severe weather. Playing on a field that has been closed by the field owner/manager is strictly prohibited and is considered a violation of DC Soccer Club policy.

Notifications and Communication

DC Soccer Club staff will make best efforts to communicate modifications and cancellations ninety (90) minutes in advance of a scheduled activity, in accordance with the above policies; however, staff may cancel activities, should fields be closed or weather conditions change and present safety risks to participants prior to or during scheduled activities.

DC Soccer Club or its coaches will make best efforts to communicate cancellations by sending emails/alerts via the PlayMetrics platform to affected players, coaches, managers, and/or teams. 

For DC fields managed by DPR, participants can sign up for email and text alert notifications by going to their website.

Policy Enforcement

Any coach who does not follow the Severe Weather Policy will be in violation of the Coach’s Code of Conduct and subject to disciplinary action. Any parent/guardian who does not adhere to this policy will be considered in violation of the DC Soccer Club Parent/Guardian Code of Conduct and/or subject to disciplinary action. Any player who does not adhere to this policy will be in violation of the DC Soccer Club Player Code of Conduct, which may result in disciplinary action.

 The league includes two competition levels:

 * Girls Academy (GA) - Top Tier
 * Girls Academy (GA) ASPIRE - Second Tier

 GA ASPIRE was introduced in the 2025-2026 season to expand access to the GA platform and provide more clubs the opportunity to compete within its standards and structure while providing a clear merit-based pathway to GA. During this season, there were over 100 clubs across the nation competing in GA ASPIRE.

 The ASPIRE level maintains a strong developmental focus while providing:

 * Regional league play
 * Access to national GA events
 * College scouting visibility
 * A structured pathway within the Girls Academy ecosystem ensuring clubs are placed in the most appropriate tier while allowing room for advancement over time.

### Travel Program And Tryout Faqs

TRAVEL PROGRAM AND TRYOUTS - FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

 At what age can players be considered for the travel program? Our travel program begins at U8 and continues to run through U19. Beginning in the 2026-2027 season, Travel age groups will now be based on the age-group cutoff of August 1-July 31, not birth years (January 1-December 31). For rising U8 players in the 2026-2027 travel season, eligibility will be August 1, 2018 - July 31, 2019. What is the eligibility by each age group for the 2026-2027 season?US Soccer has moved from January 1-December 31 (birth years) to the cutoff of August 1-July 31 for  each age group beginning in the 2026-2027 season. Below is the eligibility for each age group for the 2026-2027 season: U8: players born between August 1, 2018 - July 31, 2019U9: players born between August 1, 2017 - July 31, 2018U10: players born between August 1, 2016 - July 31, 2017U11: players born between August 1, 2015 - July 31, 2016U12: players born between August 1, 2014 - July 31, 2015U13: players born between August 1, 2013 - July 31, 2014U14: players born between August 1, 2012 - July 31, 2013U15: players born between August 1, 2011 - July 31, 2012U16: players born between August 1, 2010 - July 31, 2011U17: players born between August 1, 2009 - July 31, 2010U19: players born between August 1, 2007 - July 31, 2009Can players be placed on teams outside of their eligible school year?Players will register for the Academy ID sessions or Travel Tryouts according to their eligible age band of  August 1-July 31.  DC Soccer Club players are expected to compete in their designated age group under the age group  cut-off.In exceptional cases, coaches and technical staff may determine a player to play up an age group because it is developmentally appropriate and in the best interest of that player’s development based on skill level, maturity and overall development needs.Are there any requirements to try out or to be selected to a team?Registration is free and open to all players. In many age groups, we have between 2-5 teams that participate in leagues at various competitive levels. The goal of player placement during our ID Sessions and Tryouts is to have all players placed on teams at the level best suited for their development, where they have balanced opportunities to successfully contribute and be challenged to aid in their growth.  For our  Academy ID Sessions, all DC Soccer Cub current Academy players will attend. DCSC players currently playing on other travel  levels must be invited by their coach or the technical staff to register and attend. Academy ID Sessions are open to all players from outside of the club. All players MUST register for tryouts to be selected to a team.Do players have to register or can they just show up for the try out?All players attending tryouts (new and current) MUST register as it is the only way to ensure evaluation and communication of selection. Players should register ahead of the tryout. Any players not registered at the field will need to take the time at the field to register, which can potentially limit their time trying out.Do I register for ID Sessions (Academy Level) or Travel Tryouts?ID Sessions are for the top level Academy teams and will begin in March. Please note, these teams may not be immediately formed after an ID session. ID Sessions are used as an opportunity for staff to identify and evaluate talent both internally (current DCSC players) and externally (players from outside clubs who are interested in playing for DCSC). Therefore, players should not expect communication about offers, roster spots, playing level, team formation, etc. at the conclusion of an Academy ID Session. Our club may host additional Academy ID Sessions for specific age groups in the spring to work through the identification process when forming teams.  The rest of the travel teams below the Academy level will be held as Travel Tryouts by age group, where players selected from the tryout will be placed on the various level teams according to their current ability.Players not currently playing in DC Soccer Club’s travel program may register for either or both Academy ID sessions and Travel Tryouts. For players currently in DCSC’s travel program: Our travel program has been tracking individual player development on every team and we use this tracking system to properly evaluate players throughout the year. This typically offers a more comprehensive indication of the individual’s current competitive level, and is shared amongst the technical staff and age group directors to more accurately place players within our travel program.The vast majority of players interested in our travel program will attend our Travel Tryouts, which is held by age group. As we have several teams in each age group, these tryouts aim to select players and place them on the appropriate level team in the age group based on their current development. These tryouts will take place in April and May.What if my player has a conflict with the tryout schedule? We understand sometimes an injury, or prior important commitment, may present a challenge to attend the scheduled ID session or tryout. Please contact Technical Director Edu de Souza  about the possibility to attend a training session for evaluation.

 PLAYER PATHWAY

 DC Soccer Club's Player Pathway is designed to simplify the process for parents to better understand the varying programs levels of expectations, commitment and competition and how it connects to player development. Our competitive levels of Academy, Travel and Select are all under the travel structure, led by travel coaches and curriculum to meet players at where they are to drive player development.Placing Players at the Right Level for their Development Based on their CommitmentFamilies should first consider the expectations and commitment of each level. The competitive level is decided through a tryout process, where professional travel staff place players according to their current level of development to ensure the player is at the right level of challenge/success to develop. REC: The foundation of our club and of our Player Pathway, our
 Rec League
 is open to all ages and abilities to enjoy from Pre-K - High School with a lower commitment level and led by volunteer coaches. Registration-based program.PRE-TRAVEL: Our
Pre-Travel Academy is for U6-U8 players seeking more competitive development than rec and to prepare for travel, led by professional coaches. Registration-based program under our Programs structure.SELECT: At the base of our competitive pathway in our travel structure for U8-U14 players, it bridges the rec and travel levels with professional coaching, curriculum for development and player tracking. The focus is on player development at a level appropriate for the player, but recognizes a lesser commitment level than the travel and academy levels.
TRAVEL: The competitive travel level in our travel structure offers multiple levels/teams in each age group based on player's current competitive level, with clear expectations of the player's commitment to develop from U8-U19. Tryout-based program.ACADEMY: At the top of our Player Pathway, the Academy is for the top team in each age group U11-U19 that plays in our most competitive league (ECNL RL) and requires the highest commitment level from the top talent. Invitation and tryout-based program.U8 Players in the PathwayU8 players have several choices in the club for development outside of rec. ### Recreational Programs

RECREATIONAL PROGRAM

 DC Soccer Club is proud to offer the District’s largest youth sports program, with more than 3,500 players participating in our
Recreational Programs each fall and spring. Our goal is simple: provide a place for players of all abilities to learn and love the game in a fun, safe, and inclusive environment.What sets DCSC’s recreational pathway apart is the balance of
community-driven, volunteer-led programming alongside professional support and development opportunities. Whether you’re joining your first soccer team in Pre-K, playing Futsal indoors during the winter, or adding extra training through clinics and camps, there is a program designed for every player and family. Our recreational programs are: Accessible: Low-cost, neighborhood-based programming across DCInclusive: Coed and girls-only options; all ability levels welcomeSafe: Background checks for volunteers; clear player-safeguarding standardsVolunteer Support: Curriculum, education, and licensing pathways for volunteer coachesFlexible: Different program formats fit every family’s schedule and commitment level
Whether your child is touching the ball for the first time or building skills for the future, DC Soccer Club’s Recreational Programs provide a fun, welcoming, and development-appropriate environment for every player. RECREATIONAL LEAGUE
Our flagship Recreational League serves coed players from Pre-K through Grade 12 and a girls-only league for Grades 1–12.
Nearly half of all recreational players are girls, making DCSC the city’s largest youth sports program for girls. One neighborhood-based practice each week, plus games across DC on SaturdaysPlayers are grouped by school and neighborhood, with requests honored when possible to maximize convenience and social connectionVolunteer coaches are at the heart of this program, and are equipped with curriculum, coaching education, and development opportunities. Age Group Commissioners—also volunteers—help guide league operations across each age group.
 PRE-K PLUS PROGRAM
The
Pre-K Plus Program bridges the gap between our introductory Tots Program and the larger Recreational League. Each Saturday session combines both professional instruction and team-based play: Sessions begin with foundational skill work led by a professional DCSC coach.Players then transition to small-sided games coached by trained volunteers.
This hybrid model allows our youngest players to build confidence and friendships while taking their first steps into the team-sport environment. WINTER FUTSAL
When the weather turns cold, our
Winter Futsal League keeps players active and engaged to play outdoors in a games-only league format (no practices) on Saturdays. Using futsal’s unique format—smaller teams, heavier ball, and quicker play—players develop agility, creativity, and decision-making in a fun, fast-paced environment. CLINICS AND CAMPS
For players looking to build skills beyond weekly Rec practices or games, DCSC offers
clinics
throughout the year led by professional coaches. During school breaks and throughout the summer, DCSC runs part-day and full-day
camps. These camps mix high-quality instruction with the joy of playing with friends all day long. Players of all levels are welcome, and camps are staffed by professional coaches who combine technical training, small-sided play, and plenty of fun.TOPSOCCER FOR PLAYERS WITH DISABILITIESTOPSoccer at DC Soccer Club is an inclusive program designed for children with
physical, cognitive, and emotional disabilities to experience the joy of soccer in a
supportive, fun environment. Players develop skills, confidence, and friendships while participating at their own pace under the guidance of trained coaches and volunteer buddies.

 Travel Fees and Financial Aid

 The cost of travel soccer varies depending on age group and level. When selected players receive an invitation to a travel team, they will receive the specific fee associated with their team and what it includes.
Unlike many clubs that separate “club fees” and additional “team fees,” DC Soccer Club’s Travel Program fees are structured as an all-inclusive club fee—making it easier for families to anticipate costs throughout the season and pay through consistent monthly installments.
For the
2026-2027 travel season, travel program costs are approximately between $1700 - $3500 depending on Select, Travel or Academy level.
For more specific information by level, please go to
Program Information.
The fee for DC Soccer Club’s travel program follows an all-inclusive club fee model and includes the following in its pricing: Coaching and club staff salariesSelect tournamentsState cup competitions for eligible teamsLeague feesWinter programmingGoalkeeper TrainingState association feesPlayer carding/registration feesField and facility feesInsuranceReferee fees
PlayMetrics platformPlayer and coach development
Teams may elect to participate in additional tournaments not already included in the club fees, and those costs would be split by the amount of players on the team and incurred separately.
Uniforms are also purchased separately by players outside of the club fees, generally between $250-$350 for a 2-year cycle. The fee for travel soccer may be paid once or in monthly installments, and customized payment plans may be available upon request. All players are required to pay a non-refundable deposit upon accepting an offer to a team. FINANCIAL AIDOur club is committed to ensuring that all players can participate in travel soccer regardless of economic status. As such, we offer a generous financial aid program for qualifying families, and this includes assistance for uniforms. Please note, financial aid must be applied for annually and submission of an application does not guarantee financial aid assistance.We encourage families to apply for financial aid as soon as possible when registering for tryouts. This will allow time for the club to review qualification ahead of the tryouts, so that any players receiving offers to join the team will be able to accept in a timely manner with an up front understanding of what the cost will be. There is no obligation by applying for the financial aid, and you'll be notified within five business days.All players interested in applying for Financial Aid, please go here for more information and to apply: https://www.dcsoccerclub.org/financial-aidPlease make sure you list all of your family members; otherwise your application may be denied.

 We offer three distinct levels in our travel program for players  -- Select, Travel, and Academy--to find the right fit based on their commitment and development.
Please see our
Program Information
to understand the difference between each level. Academy
 teams are the highest level in each age group U11-U19.
At our
Travel
 Level (the middle tier of our travel program) U8-U19, we typically have between 2-5 teams in each age group to offer players the right fit for their current development with the opportunity for growth. Within each age group, DC Soccer Club forms multiple teams that are organized into
tiered competitive level
to ensure players are placed on a team with other similar level-players that meets their appropriate developmental level and play in the right league for competition. At the
Select

Our structure allows players to grow within the club at the pace that best suits their development. * All current DCSC Academy players (players on our Pre-ECNL and ECNL RL teams).

* All current DCSC U10 Boys Blue Team players (rising U11 for the 2026-2027 travel season).

* **\*\***DCSC travel players from others levels not listed above must be invited by travel coaches and/or the technical staff to register and attend.

* Players from other clubs interested in the Academy level may register and attend.

\*\*Please note our travel program has been tracking individual player development on every team and we use this tracking system to properly evaluate players throughout the year. This typically offers a more comprehensive indication of the individual’s current competitive level, and is shared amongst the technical staff and age group directors to more accurately place players within our travel program.

### Age Group Change

## AGE GROUP CHANGE

**Beginning in Fall 2026**, **DC Soccer Club will align its Travel Program age group registration with the age group cut off of August 1 – July 31**, replacing the current birth-year or calendar-year model of January 1 – December 31.

This change is in alignment with the updated guidance from US Soccer’s national member organizations US Club Soccer, US Youth Soccer and AYSO, and will be implemented uniformly by leagues and clubs across the country. As a member club, DC Soccer Club has to follow the age group change.

**Tryouts held in Spring 2026** to form teams for the 2026–2027 Travel season will be organized using the new August 1 – July 31 age cut-off for registration. Information about tryouts will be released in February.

Please note registration is still based on date of birth, not grade level in school.

Please read our [**FAQs**](https://www.dcsoccerclub.org/age-group-change#AgeGroupChangeFAQs)   for more information, and use the chart below to find your player's birthdate and corresponding age group for the 2026-2027 travel season.  If families have questions about these changes that are not addressed in our FAQs, please reach out to your [Age Group Director](https://docs.google.com/document/d/1091pvY5vdeq8BFADoa7gcYj0dMvUm4f4WH7jmGxt9A0/edit?usp=sharing).

## AGE GROUP CHANGE FAQs

* #### Can players be placed outside their age group in order to stay with their current team?

 Players will register for tryouts and will be placed in their designated age group.

 Players will not “play up” in an effort to remain with their current team. Players will play on-age in accordance with the updated school-year age cut-off.

 **Technical staff only can recommend a player play with an older age group if it is clearly in the player’s best developmental interest. This is not the parents or player’s decision.**

 Please note that the U18 and U19 age groups are combined in our travel leagues and listed as U19.

* #### How will travel program tryouts be affected?

 **Player placement from tryouts for the 2026-2027 season will follow the new age group cut off of August 1 - July 31.** Players and families will receive guidance and updates ahead of tryout registration.

* #### Why is this change happening?

 First, the Aug. 1 age group cut-off most effectively reduces the number of “trapped players” in youth soccer and reduces negative impacts on these players at multiple stages of their career. “Trapped” players are those who, due to soccer age group cut-offs, are in an older soccer age group than their school year. Trapped players have significantly reduced competition opportunities and disrupted training experiences in 8th grade (when most of their team is in high school) and are similarly negatively impacted four years later when players from the older soccer age group, but who are still in high school, are moved down into the same soccer age group.

 Second, an Aug. 1 age group cut-off best aligns soccer age groups with school year ages, maximizing the number of players who participate in soccer programs with their school-year friends. This alignment has a positive correlation with increased numbers of youth players entering and staying in the sport.

 This decision to move to the Aug. 1 cut-off reverts to the same age group cut-off used prior to 2017. In 2017, U.S. Soccer mandated the  Jan. 1-Dec. 31 (birth year) timeframe. Prior to 2017, youth soccer registration followed the school year model; this is a return to that model.

* #### Will travel registration be based on grades?

 **Registration is based on date of birth, not grade level.** Players’ eligibility for grades in school vary depending on the state and school system. After research and discussion, US Club Soccer, US Youth Soccer, and AYSO jointly announced a move to the school-year cut off of August 1 - July 31 to best reduce the misalignment between age and school year.

* #### Does this affect all youth soccer leagues?

 DC Soccer Club’s Academy, Travel and Select teams participate in as part of the travel program will move to the school year cut off August 1 - July 31.

 USYS, US Club and AYSO – umbrella organizations under the governing body of the US Soccer Federation –made a joint announcement of changing to school year age groups. All USYS and US Club affiliated leagues– national, regional and state–will have registrations of the school year age group August 1 - July 31. This includes ECNL and ECNL Regional Leagues, Girls Academy, EDP, NCSL as well as MSYSA leagues such as MDSL.

 MLS Next announced their MLS Next Academy (second tier) would align registration to the school year cut off August 1 - July 31, while the MLS Next Home Grown (first tier) will remain as birth years.

* #### Will other DC Soccer Club programs use the school-year age group cutoff besides the Travel program?

 DC Soccer Club will follow the August 1 - July 31 school year age band to be in alignment with the competitive leagues operated under our affiliate organizations of USYS and US Club. **DC Soccer Club’s recreational league and other programs will not be affected and will remain as it has been.**

 While DCSC’s Pre-Travel Academy for U6-U8 players is considered a bridge program between rec and travel to prepare players for the travel level, the registration age group will continue to be the same as rec.

* #### Will this affect high school soccer eligibility?

 No. High school soccer eligibility is governed by school athletic associations, not club organizations.

## 2.9 Clinics

### Clinics

DC Soccer Club offers training clinics for supplemental development, giving players and adults the chance to sharpen skills and gain confidence outside of their regular team environment. Clinics provide focused attention, extra repetitions and professional coaching to help players build technical ability, decision-making, and enjoyment of the game.

Throughout the year, we offer a range of clinic opportunities that may focus on technical skills like receiving, dribbling, or finishing, or provide specialized training such as goalkeeper development. Clinics are open to recreational players who want to build a stronger foundation, travel players looking for more targeted growth, and even adults who want to learn or build skills, scrimmage, and enjoy the social side of soccer.

We also host flexible formats in the off season such as drop-in sessions, where players of all levels come together for organized free play with no coaching. At other times, we offer specialty clinics that target areas like shooting to improve confidence around the goal area, or sports performance to develop the athlete and improve confidence on the soccer field by training speed, agility, coordination, balance and strength.

### Ball Mastery Clinic

Developing comfort, confidence, and precision with the ball is one of the most important steps in a player’s soccer journey. The Ball Mastery Clinic focuses on improving foot skills—the foundation of every training session and a key to success at any level. By strengthening a player’s touch, they can unlock better control, decision-making, and enjoyment of the game.

Training is designed around the individual needs of each player, with a focus on:

* **Technical Skill Development:** Coaches teach players how to move with and receive the ball so they can handle game situations with greater confidence.
* **Creative Freedom:** Sessions encourage players to try new skills, take risks, and apply techniques in a supportive environment that gradually increases pressure.
* **Small-Sided Games:** Players practice applying their technical skills in realistic, game-like scenarios that also build awareness of team play.
* **Repetitions:** Frequent touches on the ball help players improve proficiency and consistency, creating a strong foundation for growth.
* **ELIGIBILITY****:**  U8-U16 players

* **PROGRAM DETAILS:**
* 6 weekly sessions; 1 hour each
* Geared towards travel/select level players, open to all
* Players should bring a ball and water, and dress appropriately for the weather

* **COST****:**  $225+ processing fees
* **CONTACT:** Program Administrator [**Gabi Wlassits**](mailto:gabiwlassits@dcsoccerclub.org)

### Adult Clinic

DC Soccer's Adult Clinic welcomes adult players of all levels—whether you are brand new to soccer or looking to sharpen your skills. Open to ages 18+, this program features 90-minute sessions led by professional staff who provide a supportive, high-energy environment to learn, develop, and enjoy the game.

Sessions focus on building technical skills like passing, dribbling, shooting, and ball control, along with developing tactical awareness through engaging drills and small-sided games. Participants get plenty of touches on the ball and a good workout, while connecting with others in a fun and social setting.

Whether you’re stepping onto the field for the first time or rediscovering the game, the Adult Clinic offers an enjoyable way to grow your skills and appreciation for soccer.

* **ELIGIBILITY****:**  Adults 18+, all levels
